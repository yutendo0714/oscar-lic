## Immediate objective

Post-G4 conditional-go objective:

1. keep the frozen MLIC++ base and actual-byte `.oscr` accounting fixed;
2. replace raw int8 latent residual tensors with compact utility-trained residual/codebook symbols;
3. train a lightweight utility-aware gate/head against the multi-teacher objective;
4. move from residual-magnitude candidate selection to OCR-utility candidate labels and image-conditioned within-image ranking with compact/shared local latent-CNN patch encoders rather than wide flat vectors;
5. use the locked PaddleOCR recognizer-only and detector+recognizer evaluators as held-out no-regression evidence, while avoiding any PaddleOCR-driven selector tuning;
6. replace residual-MSE codebooks with utility-trained compact symbols and test whether Eval300 K64 can keep its PARSeq gain while closing the Tesseract gap;
7. run sampled interaction/Shapley diagnostics on true latent enhancement candidates once they are non-additive.
8. promote candidate-conditioned assignment as the immediate highest-leverage target: learned actual-bitstream assignment already helps at unchanged payload size, but still leaves most of the top-8 oracle headroom unused.
9. use the nearest-plus-top8 latent-window artifact as the assignment substrate, but train against policy-critical errors directly: rank-0/no-headroom suppression and hard-positive recall under high nearest-Tesseract-distance; promotion must beat `-8` Tesseract edits versus nearest, ideally with paired-bootstrap CI not touching zero, while keeping PARSeq unchanged and false/wrong changes near the current `1/75` precision floor.
10. export the real-seed cross-fit train-tuned group-gate policies and test them through counted `.oscr` plus row-wise OCR before any promotion claim.
11. design an explicit per-section recovery or UEP/FEC profile before claiming robust base decode after enhancement-layer damage.
12. use CRNN/ABINet only as train-teacher auxiliary diagnostics or regularizers inside a richer verifier; do not use their confidence argmax, teacher-loss argmax, fixed teacher-loss veto overlays, source-pseudo-label loss argmax, hard loss-no-worse filtering, sparse edit improvements, or fixed teacher string-consensus rules as a primary selector target.
13. do not tune deployable thresholds on the N060 held-out Tesseract audit categories; use N060 only to motivate richer candidate-conditioned code-effect evidence and first-stage shortlist coverage.
14. do not pursue AND/OR/consensus rules over the current pooled image-diff gate family after N068; consensus ties the current best and union adds false/wrong neutral changes rather than top-4 shortlist headroom.
15. treat the TextOCR/PaddleOCR full-image smoke as protocol plumbing only; O002 still needs license approval, frozen dataset scope and an official/pinned polygon matching protocol before publication-scale detector upper-bound claims.
16. reject independent additive top-k utility for multi-candidate enhancement after N069; future allocators must estimate conditional/diminishing-return utility or explicitly evaluate subsets.
17. after N070, require a learned or analytic stop/continue decision before adding second/third enhancement candidates; PSNR monotonicity is not evidence of OCR monotonicity.

A future compact-symbol policy must either learn candidate-local exceptions or change the symbol/proposal distribution.
23. after N138-N141, utility-positive exception centers are promising only with a materially stronger verifier. Appending K16 centers to K64 and leaving assignment as nearest produced real compact `.oscr` streams with `75` selected candidates but `0` assignment changes; simple distance/center-utility exception selectors either no-op or leak many validation no-headroom false changes; the most conservative actual OCR smoke lowers rate by selecting fewer candidates but worsens Tesseract aggregate versus current. The next attempt must add candidate-local visual/code-effect evidence before any OCR smoke.
24. after ADR-044, exception-center work should be framed as current-preserving replace/drop selection, not sparse exception-only selection. A lower-rate policy that drops current enhancements without matching current OCR is not a main OSCAR utility result.
25. after N143, the current top8 exception-center substrate contains current-selected groups only. Future work should not look for add-exception gains in this artifact; it should decide which of the `22/75` validation replacement candidates are useful and which of the `53/75` current-only rows must be kept.
26. after N144, do not train directly on the rel<=1.0 proxy-positive replacement cohort. The train split has too few proxy-false replacement negatives to teach the validation no-headroom boundary.
27. after N145, treat current-relative labels as the next audit target: the executed policy has only `5/150` strictly beneficial rows and `8/150` harmful or mixed rows, with most Tesseract harm from dropping current selections. Any replacement/drop verifier must first prove it can keep useful current rows and reject harmful replacements/drops before another actual `.oscr` OCR smoke.
28. after N146, do not use N143 proxy true/false or exception distance as a sufficient replacement/drop target. The immediate safety issue is current-only drop supervision, especially IAM hard negatives; build counterfactual labels or hard negative drop/replacement examples before training a verifier.
29. after N148/ADR-045, do not train an exception replace/drop verifier from the current N143 train current-only rows alone. Train lacks close current-only hard negatives (`14/180` at `rel<=1.5` versus validation `53/53`), so hard-negative mining or new counterfactual labeling must come first.
30. after N151, the first mined train-use probe labels are promising but not enough for training: aggregate Tesseract improves at lower rate with PARSeq unchanged, yet `4/32` rows are harmful. Use these as hard-negative diagnostics and collect more close current-only probes before fitting a verifier.
31. after N152, expanding close current-only/IAM controls to 53 actual-labeled train-use probes strengthens the exception-center branch: lower actual bpp and aggregate Tesseract improvement survive, with PARSeq unchanged, but `4/53` harmful rows remain. Treat replacement as the promising action and drop-current as a conservative rate-saving action; do not train a deployable verifier until harmful rows are explicitly modeled or filtered by candidate-local evidence.
32. after N153, the harmful boundary is small enough to become a named hard-negative set. A diagnostic verifier should be judged first on rejecting the four explicit harmful rows while preserving the replacement gains; do not optimize only aggregate Tesseract delta.
33. after N154, simple luma/diff/rate veto features show a safety/utility frontier: zero-harm is possible but overly conservative, while a one-harm budget improves aggregate Tesseract beyond baseline. Future verifier losses should expose this frontier explicitly instead of optimizing a single threshold.
34. after N155, do not train a learned exception replace/drop veto from the 53-row N152/N153 probe set alone. It is too small and too imbalanced: leave-one-out logistic/forest scorers collapse to all-veto or no-op. More labeled hard negatives or a substantially stronger objective are required.
35. after N156/N157, do not keep widening close-current thresholds just to increase probe count. Rel<=4 adds 10 training-use rows over N152 but only one additional beneficial and one additional harmful label, while zero-harm veto performance weakens slightly. The explicit expansion comparison shows added rows alone worsen Tesseract unicode by `+1`. Use N152 rel<=2 as the active exception probe frontier and treat N156 rel<=4 as auxiliary hard-negative/neutral evidence.
